# Comparison of Naive RAG vs Sentence-Window Retrieval

## Introduction
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) integrates external knowledge into large language models to improve response accuracy. Two retrieval strategies are common: naive RAG and sentence-window retrieval, each with different granularity and precision trade-offs.

## Naive RAG
Naive RAG retrieves fixed-size, large text chunks directly from a document database to provide context for the language model. While simple and easy to implement, this approach often mixes unrelated information in the retrieved chunks, leading to potential context imprecision and information overload. This can reduce the accuracy and relevance of generated responses and increase hallucinations.

## Sentence-Window Retrieval
Sentence-window retrieval breaks documents into individual sentences with their immediate surrounding context, enabling highly granular extraction of relevant information. By focusing on smaller, contextually coherent windows, this method enhances retrieval precision, reduces noise, and aligns the generation more closely with user queries. This is particularly beneficial in fields requiring fine detail, such as legal or medical domains.

## Summary Comparison
- Naive RAG uses large fixed chunks which can mix irrelevant info, reducing precision.
- Sentence-window retrieval uses sentence-level windows with context for finer-grained, more accurate retrieval.
- Sentence-window retrieval helps reduce hallucinations and improves relevance in complex domains.

## Conclusion
Sentence-window retrieval advances the naive RAG approach by enabling more precise, context-sensitive retrieval, improving the quality of generated responses especially where detail and accuracy matter.
