# Comparison Report: Vélez-Málaga vs Motril (Updated 2026)

## Introduction
This updated report compares Vélez-Málaga and Motril, two towns in Andalusia, Spain, focusing on property prices, cost of living, quality of life, and future perspectives using the most recent data available for 2026.

## Property Prices

### Vélez-Málaga
- The average property price is approximately €2,700 per square meter in 2026.
- Price varies significantly by neighborhood, with some premium areas commanding higher prices.
- This represents a healthy growth trend from previous years.

### Motril
- Estimated average property price ranges between €1,400 and €1,600 per square meter in 2026.
- Prices have shown moderate growth but the market remains more affordable compared to Vélez-Málaga.

## Cost of Living

### Vélez-Málaga
- Cost of living remains about 7% below the Spanish national average.
- The town offers affordable housing costs relative to larger cities and good access to amenities.

### Motril
- Motril traditionally maintains an affordable cost of living, consistent with its economic profile within the Granada province.

## Quality of Life

### Vélez-Málaga
- Combines Andalusian cultural heritage with ongoing urban regeneration.
- Well-serviced by healthcare facilities, schools, parks, and community amenities.
- Considered family-friendly and attractive for long-term residents.

### Motril
- Larger city offering vibrant cultural activities, beaches, parks, and rich history.
- Maintains ties to its fishing heritage while developing modern urban features.

## Future Perspectives

### Vélez-Málaga
- Focus on culture, innovation, sustainability, and economic diversification beyond tourism.
- Active urban development in logistics, industrial, and tertiary sectors.

### Motril
- Strategic development of port facilities and blue economy initiatives.
- Balancing economic growth with community and environmental preservation.

## Conclusion
Vélez-Málaga continues to display stronger property price growth, supported by cultural richness and modernization projects, while Motril offers a more affordable but growing market with considerable cultural and economic potential. Investors and residents should consider budget, lifestyle preferences, and long-term outlooks when choosing between the towns.
